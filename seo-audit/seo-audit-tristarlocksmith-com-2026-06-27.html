Executive Summary

Tristar Locksmith sits on a genuinely strong technical foundation — 162 programmatic service+city pages with unique, non-spun content, comprehensive JSON-LD (Locksmith, WebSite, Service, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, BlogPosting), an AI-ready llms.txt with full markdown mirrors, clean flat URLs, and analytics fully wired. That foundation is currently being held back by three high-impact, fixable defects. First, every one of the ~200 indexable pages renders a duplicated brand suffix in its title tag — the homepage title is "24/7 Locksmith Knoxville, TN | Tristar Locksmith, Knoxville, TN | Tristar Locksmith, Knoxville, TN" (97 chars) and money pages repeat "Tristar" three times — because the root layout's title template re-wraps a string that buildMetadata has already suffixed. Second, /robots.txt returns HTTP 404 (the [serviceCity] catch-all swallows it), so crawlers get no directives and no sitemap pointer from robots. Third, the 27 city hub pages are thin (~350 words, no FAQ) and — critically — do not link to the very money pages they should feed (e.g. /service-areas/knoxville links to the generic /services/car-lockout, never to /car-lockout-knoxville), leaving 162 pages internally orphaned. Fix these three plus the empty blog and the double-encoded meta descriptions, and this site is positioned to outrank most of the Knoxville locksmith field on white-hat fundamentals alone.

Glossary

TermDefinition
SEO (Search Engine Optimization)Optimizing pages so traditional search engines (Google, Bing) rank them for relevant queries — titles, headings, content, links, schema, technical health.
GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)Optimizing so AI answer engines (ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews) understand, trust and CITE your business — entity clarity, E-E-A-T, factual density, llms.txt.
AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)Optimizing to win featured snippets, People-Also-Ask, and voice answers — concise 40-60 word answers under question headings, FAQ/HowTo schema, lists and tables.
E-E-A-TExperience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness — Google's quality signals. For a locksmith: named owner, tenure, license, real reviews, NAP.
Answer capsuleA 40-60 word direct answer placed high on the page so search/AI engines can lift it as the answer.
Money pageA page built to capture commercial-intent searches and convert (here: /{service}-{city}).
Orphan pageAn indexable page that few or no internal links point to — harder for crawlers and users to find and weaker in ranking.
NAPName, Address, Phone — must be identical everywhere (site, schema, Google Business Profile) for local trust.
Schema / JSON-LDStructured data that labels page facts for machines (business, service, FAQ, breadcrumb, review).
CannibalizationTwo+ of your own pages competing for the same query, splitting signals; fixed with clear hierarchy and internal links.
