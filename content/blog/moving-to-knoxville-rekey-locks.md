## Why Rekeying Matters When You Move

When you receive the keys to a home — whether it's a purchase or a rental — you typically receive the keys that the previous occupants used. What you don't receive is a list of everyone who had a copy.

Over the years a home is occupied, keys get copied. By contractors, by housekeepers, by family members, by previous tenants. In a rental property, that key may have been in circulation for a decade across multiple tenants and the property managers.

Rekeying immediately changes the internal pins in the lock cylinder so every previously existing key becomes useless. The hardware stays the same. The cost is low. The peace of mind is significant.

## What Rekeying Involves

A locksmith removes the cylinder from each lock, replaces the pins to match a new key, and reinstalls the cylinder. The whole process for a typical home with three or four exterior locks takes about an hour. You end up with one or two fresh keys that work on all your exterior doors and the confidence that no previous key will ever open them again.

## When You Should Replace Instead of Rekey

Rekeying is the right move for most new homeowners and renters. But there are situations where replacing the lock hardware makes more sense:

- The existing locks are worn, sticky, or unreliable
- You want to upgrade to a higher-security deadbolt (ANSI Grade 1)
- The previous hardware was damaged or tampered with
- You want to add a smart lock or keypad entry

During a same-day visit, a Tristar technician can assess your existing hardware and give you an honest recommendation. In many cases, rekeying is all you need. In others, a hardware upgrade is worth the additional cost.

## Keyed-Alike: One Key for Every Door

One of the most practical upgrades for new homeowners is having all exterior locks rekeyed to operate on a single key. Front door, back door, garage side door — one key opens everything. It's called a keyed-alike setup and it's something Tristar Locksmith handles regularly for new Knox County residents.
