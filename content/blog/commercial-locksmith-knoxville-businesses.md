## Rekeying After Employee Turnover

This is the most common commercial call we get. An employee leaves — voluntarily or not — and they had keys to the building. The responsible move is immediate rekeying of every lock that key could open.

Rekeying is typically $20 to $40 per cylinder and can be done in a single visit across your entire facility. The old key becomes permanently useless. Your other staff keep their existing keys (if they haven't been compromised) or receive new ones.

Waiting to rekey after a problematic termination is a risk that isn't worth taking.

## Master Key Systems

If you manage a building with multiple tenants, a facility with different security zones, or a business where management needs universal access but staff need limited access — a master key system is the right solution.

A master key system is a hierarchy:
- **Grand master key** — opens everything in the facility
- **Master key** — opens a defined subset (a floor, a department, a building)
- **Change key** — opens only one specific door

## Lock Upgrades for Commercial Properties

Standard residential deadbolts aren't appropriate for commercial entry points. Commercial hardware is graded differently:

- **ANSI Grade 1** — highest residential/light commercial grade; appropriate for most commercial entry doors
- **Grade 2** — standard residential; not recommended for commercial main entries
- **Commercial-grade cylinders** — higher pick resistance, controlled keyways, restricted key duplication

If you're moving into a Knoxville commercial space that has consumer-grade hardware, an upgrade is worth discussing. A locksmith can assess existing hardware and recommend what's appropriate for the traffic, threat level, and door type.
