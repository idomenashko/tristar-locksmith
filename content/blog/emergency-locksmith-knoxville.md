## What Counts as a Locksmith Emergency

**Home lockout at night.** Being locked out of your house after 10 PM is an emergency, not an inconvenience. You shouldn't have to sleep in your car or impose on a neighbor because you can't find a locksmith willing to come out.

**Break-in repair.** If someone forced entry into your home or business, the door or lock may be physically unable to secure the space. This needs to be fixed before you can safely leave or sleep. [Emergency locksmith service in Knoxville](/emergency-locksmith-knoxville) covers break-in response including lock replacement and frame reinforcement.

**Failed lock mechanism.** A deadbolt that won't fully latch, a knob that spins without engaging, or a lock that's stuck in the open position — any of these leave your property unsecured.

**Key broken in the lock.** A key that snaps off inside a door lock or ignition needs extraction before anyone can use that entry point. Do not try to remove it yourself — the fragment can be pushed deeper.

**Car lockout in an unsafe location.** A parking garage late at night, a rest stop on I-40, a residential street in an unfamiliar area — these are situations where waiting until morning is not a practical option.
