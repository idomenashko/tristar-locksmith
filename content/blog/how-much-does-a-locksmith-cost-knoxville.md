These are ballpark figures. The actual quote depends on your vehicle make and model, lock brand, time of call, and location within the Knoxville service area.

## What Affects the Price

**Time of day.** After-hours and overnight calls often carry a premium. A locksmith available at 2 AM provides genuine value, and the price reflects that. A reputable company will disclose any after-hours fee before you agree to service.

**Location.** A locksmith call in Farragut or Hardin Valley may carry a small distance charge compared to a call in central Knoxville. This is normal and should be disclosed upfront.

**Vehicle or lock complexity.** A late-model BMW with a smart key system costs more to program than a standard domestic sedan with a cut key. A high-security Medeco deadbolt costs more to rekey than a standard Kwikset. Complexity is a legitimate pricing factor.

**Parts.** Rekeying is mostly labor. Lock replacement includes hardware. Car key replacement includes a blank and programming — both of which have real costs. Ask whether the quote includes all parts.

## The Bait-and-Switch Problem

Locksmith scams are unfortunately common across the country, including in the Knoxville market. The pattern: a service advertises a very low rate online (sometimes as low as $15 or $25), arrives at your location, and then inflates the price once your car or house is open — using excuses like "special locks" or "advanced techniques."

Warning signs:
- No company name or address on the service vehicle
- Refuses to give a firm quote before starting
- Pressure to pay before explaining the price breakdown
- Price changes dramatically after arrival

## Car Lockout Pricing in Knoxville

A [car lockout in Knoxville](/car-lockout-knoxville) typically runs $60 to $100 for standard vehicles during daytime hours. The job takes 5 to 20 minutes depending on the vehicle and access point. Newer vehicles with reinforced weather stripping and tighter door gaps can take longer.

If you're in a suburban area like [Farragut](/car-lockout-farragut) or [Maryville](/car-lockout-maryville), add a small distance factor. Emergency calls after 11:30 PM may carry an after-hours rate.

## Car Key Replacement Pricing in Knoxville

This is where the biggest variation exists. A basic non-transponder cut key can run $20 to $50. A transponder key — the standard on most vehicles made after 2000 — runs $100 to $200 including cutting and programming. Push-to-start smart keys and proximity fobs can run $200 to $350+.

[Car key replacement in Knoxville](/car-key-replacement-knoxville) from a mobile locksmith is almost always cheaper and faster than a dealership. Dealers often charge dealer markup on the key blank alone, plus labor, plus may require you to tow the vehicle. A mobile locksmith comes to your location with the equipment to cut and program on the spot.

## How to Get a Fair Price

1. **Ask for a quote on the phone**, including any potential after-hours or distance charges. A good locksmith gives you a number.
2. **Confirm it's all-in** — parts, labor, and any trip fee.
3. **Don't authorize work until you hear the price.** A legitimate technician won't start until you agree.
4. **Check for a local address.** Companies with a real local presence are easier to hold accountable.
