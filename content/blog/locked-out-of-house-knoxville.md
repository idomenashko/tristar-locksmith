Stepping outside for the mail and hearing the door click shut behind you. Realizing mid-commute that your keys are still on the kitchen counter. Coming home late to find you grabbed the wrong set. House lockouts happen in Knoxville every day, and most of them are resolved within the hour.

Here's what to do when it happens to you.

## First: Don't Force It

The biggest mistake people make is trying to get in themselves. Forcing a window, using a credit card on a deadbolt, or trying to shimmy a sliding door can damage the door frame, break a lock mechanism, or create an entry point that's easier for anyone to use again later. If there's no obvious easy entry, don't force it.

## Check Your Options Before Calling

Before picking up the phone:

- **Try all doors and windows.** Back doors, garage doors, basement windows — something is occasionally unlocked.
- **Call a family member or roommate** who has a spare key.
- **Check your lease** if you rent — some landlords have emergency contact lines.
- **Check your home warranty or renter's insurance** — some policies cover lockout calls.

## What the Locksmith Will Ask For

Expect to show a photo ID when the technician arrives. This is standard practice for residential lockouts and protects you: it ensures the technician is opening the right door for the right person. If you don't have ID on you, have any mail, a lease agreement, or other address-matching document ready.

## After You're In

Once inside, consider:

- **Did you leave a window unlocked?** A quick walk-through to check security is worthwhile.
- **Is your lock worn?** The technician can assess it while they're there and tell you if it's time to rekey or replace.
- **Do you have a spare?** Now is the time to cut one and leave it with a trusted neighbor or family member.
